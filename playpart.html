Hemanth Kumar acts in a play


A Doll's House Author: Henrik Ibsen

NORA. _[drops her cloak]_. Someone is coming now! _[Goes to the door and listens.]_ No—it is no one. Of course, no one will come today, Christmas Day—nor tomorrow either. But, perhaps—_[opens the door and looks out]_. No, nothing in the letterbox; it is quite empty. _[Comes forward.]_ What rubbish! of course he can’t be in earnest about it. Such a thing couldn’t happen; it is impossible—I have three little children. _[Enter the NURSE from the room on the left, carrying a big cardboard box.]_

NURSE. At last I have found the box with the fancy dress.

NORA. Thanks; put it on the table.

NURSE. _[doing so]_. But it is very much in want of mending.

NORA. I should like to tear it into a hundred thousand pieces.

NURSE. What an idea! It can easily be put in order—just a little patience.

NORA. Yes, I will go and get Mrs Linde to come and help me with it.

NURSE. What, out again? In this horrible weather? You will catch cold, ma’am, and make yourself ill.
